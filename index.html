IT TOOK ME A LONG TIME TO SEE IT TOO

I've spent years managing and leading B2B sales teams. Good people. Hard workers with full diaries.

And for a long time, when the numbers weren't right, the conversation always went to the same places. More calls. Sharper technique. Better objection handling. Work the pipeline harder.

It didn't move the needle. Not consistently. Not in the way it should have.

Then something shifted. I stopped looking at how the team was selling and started looking at where they were selling. Not where they thought they were spending their time. Where they were actually spending it.

What the data showed was uncomfortable.

A significant proportion of visiting time was going to accounts that had no realistic chance of moving the revenue numbers. Not because the salespeople were making bad decisions — but because nobody had ever given them a framework for making that decision in the first place. They were working on instinct, on habit, on relationships built over years. And instinct, habit and relationships are not a territory strategy.

So we rebuilt it from the ground up.

We ranked every account by what it was actually generating. We drew a clear line at 80% of territory revenue and let the data decide which accounts were genuinely driving the business. We made deliberate decisions about which accounts got physical time, which got managed at a distance, and which were quietly let go.

Then we took the capacity that released and pointed it at the accounts — and the prospects — that actually deserved it.

The result was 60% more revenue. From roughly half the accounts we started with.

Same team. Same territory. Same number of days on the road. Different decisions about where the time went.

That is what the 90-Day Account Plan is built on.

WHY BETTER SELLING DOESN'T FIX THIS

There is no shortage of sales training. Most of it focuses on the same things — how to open a call, how to handle objections, how to close. That training has its place.

But it doesn't fix the underlying problem.

If your time is going to the wrong accounts, better technique won't save you. You'll close more business with accounts that aren't capable of moving your numbers. The effort goes up. The results don't follow.

The reason most salespeople never identify this is that the problem is invisible from the inside. A full diary feels like productivity. A long account list feels like opportunity. Busy feels like progress.

It isn't.

The 90-Day Account Plan works differently. It doesn't start with how you sell. It starts with where you sell — and it gives you a structured, data-led way to make sure your time is going to the accounts that can actually move the needle.

It works across industries. It works for capital sales and for account-based sales. It works whether you have 50 accounts or 200. The process is universal. The figures are your own.

And if you already have a strong instinct about which accounts matter — the framework will either validate that instinct or show you something your instinct missed. Either way, you leave with clarity you didn't have before.

HOW THE FRAMEWORK WORKS

The 90-Day Account Plan works across four disciplines, in a specific order. Each one builds on the last.

  • 01

    Territory Management

    Before anything else, you need to know what your territory can realistically carry. How many accounts can you physically visit at the right frequency given the time you have? Most salespeople have never done this calculation. Once you do, it changes everything that follows — because you're working from real numbers rather than assumption.

  • 02

    Account Classification

    This is where the data does the work. You rank every account by what it's generating and let the numbers decide which accounts are driving your business — and which ones are just filling the diary. No sentiment. No history. No relationship bias. Just data. The accounts that belong above the line will be above the line. The ones that don't, won't — regardless of how long you've been visiting them.

  • 03

    Account Planning

    Once you know which accounts matter, you invest your planning time accordingly. Structured sales plans with clear commercial goals and measurable outcomes. You stop planning for everyone and start planning properly for the accounts that have earned it — which means the quality of your thinking goes up significantly.

  • 04

    Opportunity Management

    For every account, you apply a logical and systematic process that helps you see where the real opportunities are — and how to make sure you are getting the most from them. This is how you stop wasting time on deals that aren't going to close and start concentrating your effort where it will actually land.
