# MYS Ideation Skill

Video ideas for Mark Young Sales aren't generated from YouTube trends or
keyword tools. They're generated from the specific frustrations, blind spots,
and unspoken beliefs of one audience: B2B field salespeople and area managers
who are working hard and not seeing the results they expect. Every idea must
start there — with what the viewer feels — not with what Mark knows.

---

## Channel Context

**Audience:** B2B field salespeople and area managers. Typically 3–15 years
experience. They manage territories with multiple accounts. They're competent
but frustrated. They're not looking for motivation — they're looking for
clarity about why their effort isn't converting.

**Core thesis (locked):** Most sales problems are account and territory
problems, not selling problems. The channel exists to name that, build
the audience, and convert the right people into the 90-Day Account Plan.

**Delivery style:** Calm, measured, authoritative. Head of Sales — not
YouTube performance. Ideas must suit this register. No "growth hacks",
no clickbait concepts, no content that requires a high-energy presenter.

## Generating New Ideas

When the idea bank is exhausted or a fresh angle is needed, use these
four sources:

### Source 1 — The Frustration List

What do field salespeople say when they describe their week? Common language
patterns that signal a video idea:

- "I'm calling on them but nothing's moving" → account inertia / false signals
- "I don't have time for everything" → capacity / prioritisation
- "My manager wants more calls" → activity metrics vs outcome metrics
- "I know my patch well" → familiarity bias / complacency in A accounts
- "The CRM takes up half my day" → admin burden, system misuse
- "I've got a big pipeline but targets are tight" → pipeline quality vs quantity

Each frustration is a potential video. The idea is the underlying cause
of that frustration — which the viewer doesn't know yet.

### Source 2 — The Reframe

Take something the viewer thinks is positive and show it's actually a problem.
Or take something they think is a problem and show it's actually a symptom.

Reframe examples:
- "More accounts = more opportunity" → wrong. More accounts = less depth.
- "A full diary = productive week" → wrong. A full diary = reactive territory.
- "Strong relationships = strong accounts" → not necessarily. Relationship
  bias is one of the biggest blockers to rational territory management.

### Source 3 — The VOD Bridge

Every paid tool in the 90-Day Account Plan has a free video that creates
the problem-awareness for it. The rule: name the problem, don't give the
tool. Map existing VOD content to potential bridge videos:

| VOD Content | Potential Bridge Video |
|---|---|
| Territory capacity calculation | The number of accounts thing (idea 10 above) |
| Account classification (A/B/C) | The 80/20 video (idea 2 above) |
| Account Sales Plans | Why equal planning time is a mistake (idea 7) |
| Customer Opportunity Sheet | What a structured visit achieves (idea 5) |
| The Five Filters | The false signal problem (idea 4) |
| Prospect Management Tracker | The prospecting trap (idea 9) |

New bridge video ideas follow the same pattern: identify a VOD tool or
concept, then work backwards to the viewer's experience of not having it.

### Source 4 — Pattern Spotting from Performance

Once videos are live and accumulating data, the strongest future ideas come
from what's already working. Use the 7-day and 28-day performance reviews
(see channel-performance-review skill) to identify:

- Which titles are generating the highest CTR? What problem are they naming?
- Which videos have the highest AVD? What structure are they following?
- Which traffic sources are sending the most engaged viewers?

Ideas that extend the strongest performers in a similar style are the
highest-probability bets.

## Evaluating an Idea

Every idea — whether from the bank or freshly generated — must pass this
evaluation before moving to title and scripting.

### The Five Filters for Video Ideas

**1. Does it name a problem the viewer feels, not a topic they might find
interesting?**
"Territory management" is a topic. "Why your full diary might be the reason
your numbers aren't moving" is a felt problem. The viewer must recognise
themselves in the idea, not just find the subject matter relevant.

**2. Can it be executed without giving away paid methodology?**
The goal is problem-awareness, not solution delivery. If answering the
video's core question requires handing over the Account Classification
system, the Five Filters, or the specific tools from the VOD — the idea
needs reframing. Name the problem. Gesture at the category of solution.
Don't give the steps.

**3. Does it suit Mark's delivery style?**
Calm, evidence-led, measured. Ideas that require high energy, comedy,
controversy, or trend-chasing don't fit. If the idea only works with a
different presenter, it's the wrong idea for this channel.

**4. Is it different enough from what's already been published?**
Check the five locked videos and the idea bank. Different angle, different
problem, different viewer state. Variations on a theme are fine — direct
repeats aren't.

**5. Can it work as a standalone video with a clear payoff?**
The viewer should finish the video and feel they got something — a new way
of seeing their situation, a specific question answered, a realisation they
didn't have before. If the video can only land properly with prior context
from other videos, it's not ready as a standalone.

## Developing an Idea into a Brief

Once an idea passes the evaluation, develop it into a one-paragraph brief
before handing off to the mys-title-thumbnail skill.

**Brief format:**

```
IDEA: [One-sentence description of the core idea]
VIEWER STATE: [What the viewer currently believes or feels when they click]
REFRAME: [What they'll believe or understand differently by the end]
WORKED EXAMPLE ANGLE: [If Sarah Mitchell applies, how? If not, what scenario?]
BRIDGE TO OFFER: [How does this naturally lead toward the 90-Day Account Plan
without selling it directly?]
READY FOR TITLE WORK: Yes / No (and why, if No)
```

Do not pass an idea to the title-thumbnail skill without completing this brief.

## Content Pipeline Principles

**Audience before offer.** The channel's job right now is to build an
audience of people who trust Mark's perspective. Ideas that are obviously
designed to sell rather than to serve will underperform. Serve first.
The conversion follows.

**Consistency over volume.** One well-developed idea executed properly
beats three rushed ones. Don't generate ideas faster than they can be
scripted and filmed to standard.

**Bridge content, not tutorial content.** The 90-Day Account Plan is the
methodology. YouTube content builds the case that the methodology is needed.
The moment a video starts delivering the methodology for free, it's
competing with the product rather than feeding it.

**No sacred ideas.** If an idea from the bank isn't working when developed,
drop it. The list exists to give options, not to obligate execution.
